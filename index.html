Research Experience

1. Multi-Omics Integration revealing the DLC1-FOXD3 Axis in Neural Crest Development

While the RhoGAP protein DLC1 is known to regulate neural crest cell fate and migration by modulating downstream neural crest-determining markers like *SOX9*, *SNAI2*, and FOXD3, the precise mechanism governing FOXD3 regulation has remained elusive. Unlike *SOX9* and *SNAI2*, which are regulated via intronic splicing, FOXD3 is an intronless gene, suggesting an alternative regulatory pathway. To elucidate this, we performed mass spectrometry following DLC1 immunoprecipitation (IP-MS) in iPSC-derived neural crest cells. Functional enrichment of DLC1 cofactors revealed a novel role for DLC1 in mRNA stabilization through direct binding. This interaction was mapped using DLC1 RIP-seq, which demonstrated a distinct enrichment of binding signals localized around the 3' UTR of FOXD3. Actinomycin D-treated RNA decay assays confirmed that DLC1 knockdown significantly accelerates FOXD3 mRNA degradation, proving its stabilizing role during normal neural crest development. To determine how DLC1 selectively recognizes its targets, we integrated differential expression data from RNA-seq (DLC1 knockdown vs. control) with our RIP-seq data. Motif analysis of down-regulated transcripts harboring DLC1 3' UTR peaks uncovered a highly conserved sequence motif, indicating a clear sequence-specific recognition preference for DLC1. Finally, clinical database mining revealed concurrent downregulation of both *DLC1* and FOXD3 in iPSC-derived neural crest cells from Hirschsprung disease patients, underscoring the potential pathogenic risk of disrupting this post-transcriptional regulatory axis during early development.
