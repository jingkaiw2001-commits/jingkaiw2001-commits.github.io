Research Experience

1. Multi-Omics Integration of the DLC1-*FOXD3* Axis in Development

Discovered the post-transcriptional regulatory mechanism of the DLC1-*FOXD3* axis by integrating diverse multi-omics datasets, including mass spectrometry, RIP-seq, and RNA-seq. This investigation establishes how this specific regulatory interplay influences early neural crest development and maps potential mechanical links to the pathogenesis of Hirschsprung disease.

2. Spatial Mapping Algorithms for Single-Cell Transcriptomics

Developed an algorithm leveraging spatial transcriptomics data to restore spatial context to single-cell blood RNA-sequencing data. Applying this workflow led to the discovery of a distinct positive feedback loop between pericytes and B cells that actively promotes diabetic retinopathy progression.

3. Regulatory Network Modeling via Mendelian Randomization

Conducted extensive research training in statistical genetics by integrating GWAS data and multi-omics QTL datasets using Mendelian randomization approaches. Successfully constructed a highly resolved gene-splicing and methylation-gene regulatory network in ADHD patients, uncovering novel genetic and epigenetic pathogenic mechanisms.
